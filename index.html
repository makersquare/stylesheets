Center for Advanced Visual Studies

The Center for Advanced Visual Studies is a community for contemporary art in the School of Architecture and Planning at the Massachuetts Instituate of Technology.

Leaders

Krzysztof Wodiczko

Head of the Center for Advanced Visual Studies as well as Professor of Architecture at MIT, Krzysztof Wodiczko has developed a series of public intervention instruments, such as Homeless Vehicle , 1988-89, Dis-armor , 1999-2000 and AEgis , 2000, which was presented as part of the Whitney Biennial 2000.

Meg Rotzel

Program Coordinator at the Center where she manages the affiliate and fellow programs and works with visiting artists during their stay.

Joe Zane

He oversees the physical property of the Center and works closely with resident and visiting artists, coordinating and installing their presentations and exhibitions as well as managing the production of new artwork.
